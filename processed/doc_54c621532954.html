(a)

The board of commissioners agrees to consolidate its in rem tax foreclosure proceedings, as specified in this section, with any municipality within the county which shall so agree by appropriate resolution. The procedure for handling consolidated in rem tax foreclosures shall be as follows:

(1)

On or about April 15 of each year, the collector of each municipality shall send to the county tax collector a list of all tax liens held by that municipality. The county tax collector shall compare the list of the municipality's tax liens with a list of tax liens held by the county and shall prepare a roster indicating all property on which both governing bodies hold tax liens.

(2)

The county tax collector shall send a copy of the joint roster to the municipal tax collector, together with a request for such specific information as may be required to initiate foreclosure proceedings. The municipal tax collector shall furnish the specific information requested and shall enclose a copy of the authorization of the governing body to foreclose the tax liens.

(3)

Upon receipt of the necessary information from the municipal tax collector, the county tax collector shall initiate in rem foreclosure proceedings, as outlined in G.S. 105-375, on behalf of both governing bodies. The county tax collector will initiate the docketing of certificates for taxes due to the preceding year and will initiate execution of the unpaid judgments six months after docketing. The county tax collector will only initiate in rem foreclosure for city tax liens that are also held by the county.

(4)

After the docketing of certificates has been completed and before the six months have elapsed, any partial payments shall be proportioned to the amount owed to each governing body.

(5)

If it becomes necessary to employ an attorney in connection with any joint foreclosure proceeding, the county tax collector shall so notify the county attorney.

(6)

All unrecoverable costs of any joint foreclosure proceeding shall be borne jointly by the governing bodies participating in the foreclosure. The amount of the total unrecoverable costs to be borne by each governing body shall be in proportion to the relationship that governing body's lien bears to the total amount of all liens foreclosed in that particular proceeding.

(7)

The county tax collector shall have the authority, in accordance with the terms of G.S. 105-376, to bid on property sold as a result of a joint foreclosure proceeding on behalf of all governing bodies participating in the joint foreclosure under this agreement; however, the bid of the county tax collector shall not exceed the total amount of the tax liens, plus costs, on any single foreclosure. If the county tax collector is the high bidder, the governing bodies involved in the foreclosure shall own the property jointly, in proportion to the amount of the lien held by each. Should the property lie within the corporate limits and the city would wish to own this property in the entirety, the city may negotiate with the county and pay the total amount of both the county and city liens plus any additional costs.

(8)

The taxpayer will compensate the county an administrative fee of $50.00 on each account when judgment is entered for in rem foreclosures only. There is no additional fee for filing or executing judgments for the city.

(b)

The board of commissioners authorizes the county tax collector to enter into joint foreclosure proceedings with the tax collector of any municipality within the county which shall so agree by appropriate resolution, under G.S. 105-374, to foreclose tax liens by action in the nature of an action to foreclose a mortgage. It is the intention of this board that the tax collectors of the governing bodies shall employ the county attorney for the purpose of initiating such foreclosure proceedings whenever they shall jointly decide that foreclosure under G.S. 105-374 is warranted. If this foreclosure proceeding is used, the governing bodies shall jointly bear any unrecoverable costs, as provided in subsection (a)(6) of this section, of the in rem foreclosure proceedings. Subsection (a)(7) of this section of the in rem procedure, relating to joint ownership of property purchased by the county tax collector at foreclosure sales, shall also apply to foreclosures initiated under G.S. 105-374.
