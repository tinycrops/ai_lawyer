(a)

Except as hereinafter provided it is unlawful for any person to enter the courthouse without passing through the security checkpoint(s) established at the side entrance of the courthouse (College Street entrance) or at any other entrance approved by the sheriff.

(b)

Except as hereinafter provided it is unlawful for any person to enter the courthouse without first allowing the sheriff's personnel to visually inspect and x-ray any parcel, box, package, briefcase, attache case, purse or other object possessed.

(c)

The county sheriff may determine the times and dates to operate the security checkpoint(s). When the sheriff is operating the checkpoint everyone must pass through the checkpoint and submit to searches of their person and property. When the sheriff is not operating the checkpoint, it is not a violation of this section to enter the courthouse without being searched.

(d)

The director of physical facilities will place and maintain signs at each entrance to the courthouse stating that all persons and property entering the courthouse are subject to search.

(e)

Everyone entering a courtroom must submit their persons and property to a search by sheriff's personnel using specialized surveillance and detention equipment.

(f)

Penalty for violation of section. A violation of this section shall constitute a Class 3 misdemeanor, punishable by a fine of up to $500.00 or a maximum 30 day's imprisonment as provided in G.S. 14-4.
