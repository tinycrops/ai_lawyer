(a)

In keeping with the desire of the county to enact programs that are essential to reducing the number of animals entering the county's shelter and adoption center, increasing the save rate of healthy, adoptable animals, and reducing pet overpopulation, programs shall be enacted and provided to promote the spaying and neutering of dogs and cats as well as other life-saving methods.

(b)

The director shall be responsible for the development and implementation of programs for adoption and sterilization of dogs and cats as well as the authority to revise ongoing programs in order to decrease shelter intake and increase live outcome of animals, including, but not limited to, expanding the trap, neuter, and release program; creating a sterilization program targeting hard-to-adopt animals; and collaborating with other animal welfare organizations to increase public access to low-cost spay and neuter services.

(c)

Funding for such programs shall be provided through the animal services budget, grants, donations, and the Animal Services Trust Fund.

(d)

The director or his designee shall have the authority to waive or reduce any fee by the division for individuals who meet federal low-income guidelines, to promote adoptions or as a life-saving measure. The director or his designee is further authorized to establish a payment plan for those individuals that are unable to pay fees in full in order to reclaim a pet from the shelter. Each situation will be reviewed on a case-by-case basis.

(Ord. Of 9-21-21(2))
