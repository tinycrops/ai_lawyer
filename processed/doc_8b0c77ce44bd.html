(a)

Any pawnbroker license issued under this division may be revoked by the sheriff, after a hearing, for substantial abuses of G.S. 91-1 et seq., or the violation of any of the requirements of this division, including, but not limited to:

(1)

Conviction of a felony.

(2)

Submitting false information on the application.

(3)

Failure to keep proper records.

(4)

Failure to make such records available to the sheriff's office.

(b)

The hearing required by subsection (a) of this section shall be conducted by the sheriff after ten-day prior written notice is provided to the licensed pawnbroker. Such notice shall include the date, place and time of the hearing and shall specify the nature of the abuse or violation justifying the revocation of the license. Such notice shall be deemed sufficiently given if delivered to the pawnbroker personally or left at the pawnshop during normal business hours.
