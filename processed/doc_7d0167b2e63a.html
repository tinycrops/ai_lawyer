Exotic animal is any animal which:

(1)

Is not native or indigenous to North Carolina;

(2)

Does not have an established wild population in North Carolina;

(3)

Is not regulated by the North Carolina State Wildlife Commission; and

(4)

Is not considered livestock as defined in section 3-1 of this chapter.

Inherently dangerous animal means any of the following:

(1)

Any wolf, fox, coyote, or member of the family Canidae not customarily domesticated by man, including hybrids between dogs and wild species.

(2)

Any member of the cat family weighing over fifteen (15) pounds other than the domestic cat, or any member of the species Felis sylvestris regardless of size, or any hybrid of domestic and wild cats, or any member of the genus Panthera.

(3)

Any bear or hybrid of any species of bear.

(4)

Any non-human primate.

(5)

Any venomous reptile or venomous amphibian.

(6)

Any constrictor snake, including, but not limited to, pythons and anacondas.

(7)

Any crocodile, alligator, or caiman.

(8)

Any member of the family Macropodidae, including, but not limited to, kangaroos.

(9)

Any animal that is not livestock or customarily used for agricultural purposes and which weighs over three hundred (300) pounds.

Wild animal means any animal native or indigenous to North Carolina with an established wild population in North Carolina.

(Ord. Of 9-21-21(2); Ord. No. 2022-02, 3-15-22)
