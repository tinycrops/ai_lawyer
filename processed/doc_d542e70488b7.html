(a)

A potentially dangerous dog that has killed or inflicted severe injury on a pet or domestic animal can have the designation removed if:

(1)

The dog and the owner successfully pass an AKC canine good citizen test; and

(2)

The dog has no reported incidents to animal services for 36 consecutive months after designation of potentially dangerous.

(b)

It is the responsibility of the dog owner to submit a request for designation removal to animal services after 36 consecutive months and completion of AKC canine good citizen test. The potentially dangerous dog designation remains in effect until the dog owner receives written notification from animal services that it has been removed.

(Ord. No. 2023-2, 3-6-2023)
