(a)

The animal services director shall be responsible for enforcement, within the county, all public and public-local laws pertaining to the ownership and control of dogs, cats or other animals. The animal services director shall cooperate with all law enforcement officers operating within the county in fulfilling this responsibility.

(b)

The duties of the animal services director shall be, but not be limited to, the following activities:

(1)

Collect and impound stray dogs, cats, and other animals.

(2)

Canvass the county to determine if there are any animals not wearing the required rabies vaccination tag and follow the process in accordance with G.S. Ch. 130A-192.

(3)

Organize at least one countywide rabies vaccination clinic per year for the purpose of vaccinating animals in accordance with G.S. รยง 130A-185.

(4)

Maintain the county animal shelter, including required records of all animals impounded, released, and euthanized.

(5)

Upon delegation of authority by the health director as provided in G.S. 130A-6, enforce statewide rabies laws within the county, including post-exposure management, in accordance with the National Association of State Public Health Veterinarians Compendium of Animal Rabies Prevention and Control.

(6)

Regulate dogs and cats that may be a nuisance or danger to the citizens of the county.

(7)

Investigate complaints of cruelty or abuse to any animal; ensure that animals are kept in sanitary and humane conditions; provided with proper food and potable water daily; and provided shelter from weather.

(c)

The animal services director shall be subject to the supervision of the county manager.

(d)

The animal services director shall supervise all animal services officers and other county animal services employees.

(e)

The animal services director may delegate any of his powers and duties to county animal services officers or other animal services staff as needed.
