The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:

Hazardous waste means:

(1)

Any solid, liquid or gas which has been classified as hazardous waste by the division of health services of the state department of human resources or its successor, or by the Environmental Protection Agency of the United States or its successor;

(2)

Any solid, liquid or gas which, when burned or combined with any other solid, liquid or gas, would be classified as hazardous waste as provided in subsection (1) of this definition; or

(3)

Any other solid, liquid or gas which the county manager, in consultation with the health director, determines is detrimental to the public health, safety and general welfare of the citizens of the county.

Ionizing radiation means gamma rays and X-rays, alpha and beta particles, high speed electrons, protons, neutrons and other nuclear particles; but not sound or radio waves or visible, infrared or ultraviolet light.

Nonionizing radiation means radiation in any portion of the electromagnetic spectrum not defined as ionizing radiation, including, but not limited to, such sources as laser, maser or microwave devices.

Person means any individual, corporation, partnership, firm, association, trust, estate, public or private institution, group, agency, or political subdivision of this state, any other state or political subdivision or agency thereof, and any legal successor, representative, agent or agency of such entities, other than the United States Nuclear Regulatory Commission or any successor thereto, and other than federal government agencies licensed by the United States Nuclear Regulatory Commission or any successor thereto.

Radiation means gamma rays and X-rays, alpha and beta particles, high speed electrons, protons, neutrons and other nuclear particles, and electromagnetic radiation consisting of associated and interacting electric and magnetic waves, including those with frequencies between 3 times 10 to the eighth power cycles per second and 3 times 10 to the twenty-fourth power cycles per second and wavelengths between 1 times 10 to the minus fourteenth power centimeters and 100 centimeters.

Radiation machine means any device designed to produce or which produces radiation or nuclear particles when the associated control devices of the machine are operated.

Radioactive material means any solid, liquid or gas which emits ionizing radiation spontaneously.
