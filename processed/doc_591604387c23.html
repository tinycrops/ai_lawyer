After the preliminary assessment roll has been prepared and filed in the office of the clerk to the board pursuant to this article, a time and place for a public hearing shall be set. At least ten days before the date set for the hearing, the board shall publish a notice that the preliminary assessment roll has been completed. The notice shall describe the project in general terms, note that the roll in the clerk's office is available for inspection, and state the time and place for the hearing on the roll. At least ten days prior to the date set for the hearing, the county zoning administrator shall send, by certified mail, to each property owner, as determined from property tax records, a copy of the preliminary assessment roll, shall state the time and place of the hearing, and shall note that the roll in the clerk's office is available for inspection.
