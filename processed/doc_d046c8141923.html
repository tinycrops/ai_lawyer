The following activities, among others, are hereby declared to be unreasonably loud noises or sounds which are in violation of this division, but nothing in this section shall be construed as preventing other activities, noises and sounds from also violating this division:

(1)

The sounding of any horn or similar signaling device on any automobile, motorcycle, bus or other vehicle on any street or in any public place of the county except as a danger warning; the creation by means of any such horn or signaling device of any unreasonably loud or harsh sound; and the sounding of any such horn for any unnecessary unreasonable period of time. The use of any horn or signaling device, whistle or other device operated by engine exhaust or the use of any such signaling device when traffic is for any reason held up.

(2)

The playing of any musical instrument, television, radio, stereo or electronic sound amplification equipment in such manner or with such volume, particularly between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m., such that a reasonably prudent person would recognize as likely to unreasonably disturb persons in the vicinity.

(3)

The creation of unreasonably loud noises and sound levels caused by musical instruments and/or electronic sound amplification equipment during any outdoor concert, musical performance, show or other similar outdoor event such that a reasonably prudent person would recognize as likely to unreasonably disturb persons in the vicinity except with an appropriate permit issued under this division.

(4)

The use of any automobile, motorcycle or vehicle so out of repair, so modified or loaded or operated in such manner as to create unreasonably loud sounds.

(5)

The operating of any garage or service station in any residential area so as to cause unreasonably loud sounds to be emitted between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.

(6)

The creation of unreasonably loud sound levels adjacent to any school, education facility, church or court during normal operating hours, or within 150 feet of any hospital, which a reasonably prudent person would recognize as likely to unreasonably interfere with the working of such institution, provided conspicuous signs are displayed indicating that such area is a school, education facility, church, court or hospital area.

(7)

The creation of unreasonably loud noises and sound levels caused by or during the erection (including excavation), demolition, alteration or repair of any building in a residential or business district other than between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 11:00 p.m. on any day, except in the case of urgent necessity in the interest of public safety and then only after the issuance of a special permit issued under this division.

(8)

The use of any loudspeaker, drum, instrument or any electronic sound amplification equipment for the purpose of attracting attention to any performance, show or sale or display of merchandise or for advertising and solicitation purposes, except with the appropriate permit issued under this division.

(9)

The firing or discharging of any gun, fireworks, firecracker, gunpowder or other combustible substance in the street or elsewhere for the purpose of making noise or which creates frequent or long-continued noises and sounds such that a reasonably prudent person would recognize as likely to unreasonably disturb persons in the vicinity between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m., except with the appropriate permit issued under this division or by authorized law enforcement officers discharging their official duties. Properly licensed hunters pursuing game legally and in compliance with all local, state and federal regulations are not in violation of this subsection when discharging weapons toward game.
