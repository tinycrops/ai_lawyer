(a)

It shall be unlawful for any owner to permit an animal to run at large so as to create a public nuisance.

(b)

For the purpose of this section, public nuisance includes, but is not limited to, the following:

(1)

Animals that habitually or repeatedly disturb, interfere with or annoy human beings while not in the mode of attack; or

(2)

Animals that damage the property of another, defecate or urinate upon the property of another, tip over garbage pails or damage gardens, flowers or vegetables; or otherwise interfere with the reasonable use of one's property; or

(3)

In the case of a female animal, ones that are not confined in a secure enclosure during estrus; or

(4)

Dogs that are found to be without restraint, off their owner's property on a public highway, street, roadway or sidewalk.

(c)

When the animal services director's investigation determines that a violation of this section is supported by evidence, the animal services director will issue the owner or keeper a containment order notice as outlined in section 3-32, which requires the owner or keeper to-to comply with specific preventive measures for the containment of an animal or animals on his or her property at all times.

(d)

Fine for violation. After a containment order notice has been issued to a person, the animal services director, upon finding evidence to support a subsequent violation of this section, including failure to abide by the containment order notice, may issue the owner or keeper a citation with costs and fines as follows:

(1)

In addition to any fines, attorney's fees if and as incurred in the enforcement or prosecution of the violation, or as allowed by the court;

(2)

For the first subsequent violation, a fine of one hundred dollars ($100.00);

(3)

For the second subsequent violation, and any subsequent violation of this section, a fine of two hundred dollars ($200.00);

(4)

If the nuisance animal is a female in estrus, in addition to the notice of the "restraint of nuisance animals running at large" above, there shall be a fine of five hundred dollars ($500.00) imposed for each incident and each animal; and,

(5)

If a nuisance animal is spayed or neutered within thirty (30) days of the notice of the "restraint of nuisance animals running at large," the fine in subparagraph (4) shall be reduced to fifty dollars ($50.00). The fine in subparagraph (1) shall be waived. This time period may be extended at the discretion of the director of animal control if an appointment has been made within the thirty (30) day period with a licensed veterinarian and/or a spay or neuter free or reduced fee clinic to the day of the appointment. These provisions shall be verified by the licensed veterinarian performing the procedure;

(6)

Sustained containment order violations will be enforced as outlined in section 3-29(a)(1)d.

(e)

A violation of this section is a misdemeanor.

(Ord. Of 9-21-21(2); Ord. of 1-20-22(1))
