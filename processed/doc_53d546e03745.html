(a)

Application required; contents. Any person desiring to engage in the business, trade or profession of masseur or masseuse or the operation or carrying on of any of the businesses, trades, professions, occupations or callings listed in subsection (a) of section 5-26 shall, before engaging in such business, trade, profession, occupation or calling, file an application for a license, addressed to the board of commissioners. Such application shall be in writing and shall set forth the following:

(1)

Name and address of applicant. If the applicant is a corporation, the address of such corporation and its officers shall be provided.

(2)

Qualifications of applicant. These must be plainly stated and must be submitted together with required exhibits, attached to the application, proving such qualifications.

(b)

Qualifications of applicant. An applicant for a license under this division, prior to making application for a license, must have the following qualifications. The applicant may be male or female. The applicant shall be required to provide written recommendations showing proof of good moral character. If the applicant is a corporation, such corporation must be created in or domesticated by the laws of this state, and the officers thereof shall provide written recommendations showing proof of good moral character.

(c)

Health certificates. Each applicant must furnish a health certificate from a medical doctor which shall accompany such application as an exhibit. If the applicant is a corporation, it shall furnish such certificate to cover its officers, agents or employees who will be actually engaged in and working under the license. Each employee who begins work following the original issuance of a license under this division shall likewise obtain a health certificate. A copy of the application and all certificates shall be furnished to the county sheriff's department.
