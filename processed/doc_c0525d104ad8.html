(a)

In those circumstances where there is a special need for prompt action to eliminate traffic obstructions or to otherwise maintain and protect the public safety and welfare, the authorized official may order the removal of an abandoned, nuisance, or junked motor vehicle without prior notice. Findings justifying the removal of vehicles without prior notice shall be made in writing.

(b)

Circumstances that threaten public health, safety, and welfare.

(1)

For vehicles abandoned on the public streets and highways:

a.

Vehicles obstructing traffic;

b.

Vehicles parked in violation of an ordinance prohibiting or restricting parking;

c.

Vehicles parked in a no stopping or standing zone;

d.

Vehicles parked in loading zones;

e.

Vehicles parked in bus zones;

f.

Vehicles parked in designated fire lanes; or

g.

Vehicles parked in violation of temporary parking restrictions lawfully imposed under this Code.

(2)

For abandoned or nuisance vehicles left on county-owned property other than the streets and highways and on private property:

a.

Vehicles blocking or obstructing ingress and egress to businesses and residences;

b.

Vehicles parked in a manner or in a location which creates a traffic hazard;

c.

Vehicles causing damage to public or private property; or

d.

Vehicles likely to cause personal harm or injury, especially to children.
