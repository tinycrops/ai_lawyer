(a)

It is the intent of this article to prescribe regulations consistent with nationally recognized good practices for the safeguarding of life and property within the jurisdiction of the county from the hazards of fire and explosion arising from the storage, handling and use of hazardous substances, materials, devices and from hazardous conditions in the use of occupancy of buildings or premises.

(b)

This article shall not be construed to hold the county responsible for any damage to persons or property by reason of the inspection or reinspection authorized in this article, failure to inspect or reinspect the permits issued or denied as provided in this article, or by reason of the approval or disapproval of any equipment authorized in this article.
