(a)

The county manager may issue the applicant a license only when he finds that the applicant's facilities, equipment, and proposed operating methods are in compliance with this article and applicable rules of the department and that the applicant will perform solid waste collection in an efficient and sanitary manner. A condition of the license shall be that the licensee shall serve every person who contracts with him for solid waste collection in such a manner that the licensee does not cause the person to be in violation of this article.

(b)

If the county manager denies an applicant a license, the applicant may appeal the county manager's decision to the board of county commissioners by giving written notice of appeal to the county manager within two days of receipt of the county manager's decision. After a hearing on the appeal, the board shall either affirm the denial or direct the county manager to issue the license.

(c)

A license shall be valid for a period of one year from the date of issuance.
