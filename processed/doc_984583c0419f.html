(a)

Organization. The library board of trustees shall have the authority to organize itself for the transaction of business and for that purpose may:

(1)

Conduct an annual organizational meeting.

(2)

At the organizational meeting, elect a chairperson, a vice-chairperson, secretary, a treasurer or any other officer or official deemed appropriate.

(3)

Adopt bylaws, but those bylaws shall be consistent with this chapter.

(4)

Appoint standing committees as it deems necessary for the orderly transaction of business.

(5)

Establish an executive committee through its bylaws or by appointment to help administer the affairs of the trustees during those times when a meeting of the full board of trustees is not feasible.

(b)

Powers.

(1)

The board of trustees shall have the following additional powers:

a.

To formulate and suggest programs, policies and regulations for the government of the library to the library director.

b.

To make recommendations to the county board concerning the construction and improvement of buildings and other structures for the library.

c.

To establish a schedule of fines and charges for late return, failure to return, damage to and loss of library materials, fees and to take other measures to protect and regulate the use of such materials.

d.

To extend privilege and use of the library system to nonresidents of the county on the terms and conditions the trustees may prescribe.

e.

To otherwise advise the library director on all matters pertaining to the library.

(2)

The library board chairperson will appoint two members of the board to participate in an advisory capacity, in the search and interview process for a library director.

(c)

Duties. The library director shall make an annual report on the operations of the library to the board of trustees, county board, City of Morganton and the Town of Valdese and shall make an annual report to the department of cultural resources as required by G.S. 125-5.

(d)

Public body. For all purposes, the board of trustees shall be treated as a public body and therefore shall conduct its meetings in accordance with the requirements of the Open Meetings Law, the Public Bid Laws, the Public Records Law, and other laws, rules and regulation applicable to public bodies, as amended from time to time.
