(a)

This section, which is a local addition to G.S. 75A-1 et seq., requires the operator of any vessel operated on the waters of the state to stop for a blue light or siren exhibited by any law enforcement officer during the performance of duties. The section added to G.S. 75A-1 et seq. reads substantially as follows:

(1)

Operators of any vessels operated on the waters of the state are hereby required to stop for a blue light or siren exhibited by any law enforcement officer during the performance of his duties.

(2)

Every person convicted of violating this act [section] shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be punishable as if it were an offense for which the punishment is provided by G.S. 20-176(b), which states that "Unless a specific penalty is otherwise provided by law, a person found responsible for an infraction contained in this article may be ordered to pay a penalty of not more than $100.00."

(b)

This section applies only to Catawba, Gaston, Iredell, Lincoln, and Mecklenburg counties.
