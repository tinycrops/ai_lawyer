Catawba County's government by the Board of Commissioners began on August 7, 1868, when Drury Hamilton was sworn in as Chairman of the Commission by Anderson Mitchell, Judge of Superior Court. On August 10, 1868, Chairman Drury administered the oath of office to his fellow Commissioners. Those who have served since 1868 are set forth in the chronology below:

Date Commissioners
August 10, 1868 Drury Hamilton, Chairman; A. G. Corpening, A. M. Huit, A. L. Shuford and Joshua B. Little named Commissioners-Elect. They were notified to report September 7 next and qualify.
September 9, 1868 Ordered by the Board that the offices of A. L. Shuford and Joshua B. Little be declared vacant, and that the Governor be notified.
October 14, 1868 J. J. Sigmon and John D. Caldwell having been appointed by the Governor, were given oaths of office.

June 2, 1884 At the June 2, 1884, Magistrates Meeting (page 254, Minute Book #2), the Magistrates voted to elect three instead of five Commissioners. The three Commissioners, A. G. Corpening, M. M. Gabriel, and A. M. Huit, were elected to serve two-year terms beginning the first Monday in December, 1884. On December 1, 1884, A. G. Corpening was elected Chairman, and the three-member board served until December 6, 1886. At this meeting the Board organized and it became again a five-member board, with A. G. Corpening, Chairman, A. M. Huit, M. A. Abernethy, M. S. Deal, and L. R. Whitener. This board served until December 3, 1888, and there is no explanation of why the Board went back to five members in 1886 and then from 1888 to 1903 had a three-member Board.
