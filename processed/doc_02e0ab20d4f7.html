The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:

Developer means any person engaged in the development or proposed development of a subdivision, industrial park or complex, or mobile home park.

House number means the number assigned to any house, residence, dwelling, business, warehouse or other structure or property in a sequential manner.

Mailing address means the address assigned or used by the U.S. Postal Service for the purpose of delivery of the U.S. mail. The mailing address may or may not be identical to property address.

Mobile home means any vehicle, house car, camp car, or any portable or movable vehicle on wheels, skids, rollers or blocks, either self-propelled or propelled by any other means, but not including those vehicles designed primarily for the transportation of goods or people. The mobile home must have been manufactured by a recognized licensed mobile home manufacturer conforming to the standards of the Mobile Home Manufacturers Association, Trailer Coach Association or other equally recognized national association.

Mobile home park means the land leased or rented, being used, or proposed to be used by mobile homes occupied for dwelling or sleeping purposes.

Mobile home space means any parcel of ground within a mobile home park designated for the exclusive use of one (1) mobile home.

Neighborhood public roads means:

(1)

All those portions of the public road system of the state which have not been taken over and placed under maintenance or which have been abandoned by the department of transportation, but which remain open and in general use as a necessary means of ingress and egress from the dwelling house of one (1) or more families;

(2)

All those roads that have been laid out, constructed or reconstructed with unemployment relief funds under the supervision of the department of human resources; and

(3)

All other roads or streets whatsoever outside the boundaries of any incorporated city or town in the state which serve a public use and as a means of ingress or egress for one (1) or more families, regardless of whether the road or street has ever been a portion of any state or county road system.

Official name means the name of any roadway in the unincorporated area of the county, as approved by the board of commissioners, the county planning board or the county planning department.

Private mobile home park road means any street, roadway or driveway which serves two (2) or more mobile homes for residential purposes, and which has not been dedicated to the public use.

Private street means a street which is not intended to become a public street and which is to be used for access to a particular site or group development.

Property address means the house number and roadway name.

Public street means a street located on a right-of-way dedication under the requirements of this article, and which meets the total improvement requirements for a public street as set forth by the state department of transportation in its minimum construction standards for subdivision roads.

Roadway means any road, street, drive, lane, cartway, tramway, easement, right-of-way, access area, thoroughfare, highway, boulevard or any other corridor used for or having the potential use as a means of conveyance by a motor vehicle.

Roadway terms. The following shall be used after a roadway name: avenue, circle, court, drive, place, street, road, boulevard, lane, terrace.

State road number means a number assigned by the state department of transportation.

Street name means the official name of any roadway, designated by the board of commissioners or its designee.

Street sign means signage placed at the roadway intersection which indicates the street name, direction and, where appropriate, the state road number and block number.
