(a)

General. The subdivider shall submit a sketch plan prior to submitting a preliminary plat for all subdivisions subject to the major subdivision process. The purpose of submitting the sketch plan is to afford the planning board and subdivider an opportunity to obtain advice and technical assistance of the subdivision review board in order to facilitate the subsequent preparation and approval of the preliminary plat in lieu of obtaining state plan review and approvals prior to preliminary plat application. This procedure does not require a fee.

(b)

Required information.

(1)

Scale and size of plat. The sketch plan and preliminary plat shall be clearly and legibly drawn at a scale not less than 100 feet to one inch.

(2)

Plat preparation. The sketch plan and preliminary plat shall be prepared by a registered land surveyor, engineer, or landscape architect.

(3)

Development information and title block. Each sketch plan and preliminary plat shall include the following information:

a.

Subdivision name.

b.

Subdivider's name, address, and telephone number.

c.

Preparer's name, seal, address, and telephone number.

d.

North arrow.

e.

Scale (denoted graphically and numerically).

f.

Date of plat preparation.

g.

Location of subdivision (township, county, and state).

h.

Parcel identification number (PIN) of the properties to be subdivided.

i.

911 address of the principle tract (when available).

j.

Sketch vicinity map.

(4)

Property dimensions, characteristics, and uses. Each sketch plan and preliminary plat shall include the following information:

a.

The boundaries of the tract or portion thereof to be subdivided, with all bearings and distances accurately shown.

b.

Adjoining property lines, owners' names, and subdivisions of record.

c.

Total acreage in tract (and portion thereof to be subdivided, when applicable).

d.

Total number of lots.

e.

Smallest lot in square feet.

f.

Proposed lots numbered sequentially with approximate dimensions and acreage.

g.

Zoning district.

h.

Town limit lines.

i.

Existing structures and uses.

j.

Proposed use of land (if other than single-family residential).

k.

Identify adjoining nonresidential uses.

l.

Significant natural features, including wooded areas, marshes, gullies, known wetlands, major rock outcrops, lakes or streams, or other natural features affecting the site.

m.

Watershed classification (or note if not in a water supply watershed).

n.

School districts.

o.

Building setback lines (typical) and approximate building envelopes, if applicable.

p.

Fire district (note if not in a fire district).

q.

Floodplain data and flood zones.

r.

Existing topographic contours at five-foot intervals.

s.

Subsurface conditions on the tract, location and results of tests made to ascertain subsurface soil, rock and groundwater conditions, depth to groundwater, unless test pits are dry to a depth of seven feet.

(5)

Existing and proposed infrastructure and improvements. Each sketch plan and preliminary plat shall include the following information:

a.

Streets.

1.

Rights-of-way showing approximate widths and dimensions.

2.

Dedication and reservation.

3.

Pavement widths.

4.

Sight triangles.

5.

Note design standards.

6.

Proposed street names (approved names on preliminary).

7.

Curbing (when applicable).

8.

Approximate grades.

b.

Utilities.

1.

Utility easements (showing widths, dimensions, and dedication).

2.

Power lines (indicate aboveground or buried).

3.

Water and sewer lines (including distance, line size and utility stations of nearest existing and proposed).

4.

Fire hydrant locations.

5.

Street lights (including shielding and direction).

6.

Well locations and type of well.

7.

On-site wastewater systems (existing).

8.

Drainageways and easements (including width for easements).

9.

Stormwater systems.

10.

Railroads and railroad easements (including width for easements).

11.

Other applicable utility facilities and easements (including use, dedication, and size).

c.

Amenities, common areas, and other aesthetic and natural improvements.

1.

Common areas (including acreage, dedication, use, and design concepts).

2.

Trail easements (including width, dedication, use, and design concepts).

3.

Sidewalks (including easement, width, and design).

4.

Street trees, landscaping, buffers, or other natural enhancements or protections.

5.

Sign location, dimensions, and design (not to include traffic signs or street name signs purchased from the county).

6.

Areas dedicated or reserved for public service sites (i.e., school sites or fire stations) or other amenities (including use, acreage, and approximate dimensions).

(6)

Additional materials.

a.

A project narrative that provides a general theme for the development, phasing schedules, and vesting requests may be provided to clarify or elaborate on specific details shown on the plat. In addition, the narrative may include drawing and sketches of proposed facilities, amenities, or structures that may assist the subdivision review board in visualizing the proposed development.

b.

A copy of the proposed deed restrictions and restrictive covenants for the development, when required or applicable.

c.

Two copies of the draft erosion control plan for the proposed development.

(c)

The subdivision review board. The subdivision review board shall consist of the staff representatives from the following departments: planning, environmental health, general services, fire marshal, 911 addressing/land records, building inspections, the state department of transportation, and other agencies and departments as deemed necessary by the subdivision administrator.

(d)

Subdivision review board procedure.

(1)

The subdivision review board shall set regular, monthly meeting dates.

(2)

The subdivider shall submit ten copies of the sketch plan, containing all information required in subsection (b) of this section, and other applicable attachments to the subdivision administrator or his designee at least 21 days in advance of the next regularly scheduled meeting of the subdivision review board.

(3)

The subdivision administrator shall forward the complete, submitted application within three days of the deadline to the subdivision review board.

(4)

The members of the subdivision review board will prepare written comments, if any, and provide said comments to the subdivision administrator at least seven days prior to the regularly scheduled meeting of the subdivision review board.

(5)

The subdivision administrator shall provide the developer a copy of the written comments, if any, at least four days prior to the regularly scheduled meeting of the subdivision review board.

(6)

The subdivider or his agent shall attend the subdivision review board meeting to address recommendations and comments.

(7)

The subdivision administrator shall provide the subdivider final written comments and recommendations of the sketch plan review within four days after the regularly scheduled subdivision review board meeting.
