(a)

The meter shall determine the quantity of water for which the consumer shall be charged. All water passing through the meter shall be charged for, whether such water shall have been used or wasted or the premises shall not have been occupied.

(b)

Where any meter shall have been locked so that it has not registered correctly the quantity of water which has passed through since the last reading, the quantity for the month during which the meter failed to register shall be determined by taking an average of the water consumed during the three months preceding the month in which the meter has failed to register, and the consumer shall be charged for that quantity.

(c)

Meters shall be tested by the county upon reasonable requests by the consumer. The first test shall be conducted at no charge. Upon request for a second test, and subsequent tests, within a period of a year, a deposit shall be required in advance of each test. If the meter is found properly working, the deposit shall be kept by the county. If the meter is found to be out of order, the meter shall be replaced by the county and the deposit returned.

(d)

Each consumer of water shall be required to have a separate meter, provided that one meter shall be used for several premises, apartments, block of stores, shops and tenant houses where they are the properties of the same owner or group of owners. In case the ownership of one or more of such premises shall pass to another person, separate meters shall be installed.

(e)

No water shall be supplied to any customer except through a water meter approved by the county.

(f)

Ownership of water meters and connections is retained by the county.
