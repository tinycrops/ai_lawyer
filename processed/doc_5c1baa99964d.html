(a)

Unpaved streets. The county shall notify each abutting property owner, prior to the construction, of the number of water and/or sewer connections the county proposes to lay, and shall afford each owner reasonable opportunity to request that additional laterals be laid to serve undeveloped lots or tracts of land. Laterals are installed at the request of the property owners with the property owners paying all costs.

(b)

Paved streets. When a water and/or sewer main is constructed in a paved street, or in a street that is to be paved, laterals shall be laid to serve every building lot. It is the purpose and policy of the county to install sufficient laterals so as to minimize the excavation of streets in order to provide service to abutting lots which are subsequently developed and improved; provided, however, each property owner, prior to construction, shall be notified by the county of the number of laterals proposed to be laid to serve his property. The county shall give each owner reasonable opportunity to object to the installation of any of the proposed laterals. Such objections shall be filed in writing and shall contain the owner's reason for objection. The county shall have the authority to delete from the project laterals which will not be required to adequately serve the property. A property owner who objects to the decision of the county as to his objections may appeal to the board of commissioners for a final decision.
