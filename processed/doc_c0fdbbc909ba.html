In an emergency, a person who willfully ignores a warning regarding personal safety issued by a federal, state, or local law enforcement agency, emergency management agency, or other governmental agency responsible for emergency management under [this] chapter is civilly liable for the cost of a rescue effort to any governmental agency or nonprofit agency cooperating with a governmental agency conducting a rescue on the endangered person's behalf if all of the following are true:

(1)

The person ignores the warning and (i) engages in an activity or course of action that a reasonable person would not pursue or (ii) fails to take a course of action that a reasonable person would pursue.

(2)

As a result of ignoring the warning, the person places himself or herself or another in danger.

(3)

A governmental rescue effort is undertaken on the endangered person's behalf.
