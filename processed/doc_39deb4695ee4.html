(a)

A majority vote of the regular board membership (excluding vacant seats) shall be necessary to reverse an order, requirement, decision or determination of the administrator (appeal) or to grant a special use permit.

(b)

A concurring vote of four-fifths (â…˜) of the regular board membership (excluding vacant seats) shall be necessary to grant a variance.

(c)

The concurring vote of four-fifths (â…˜) of the regular board membership (excluding vacant seats) shall be necessary to reverse any order, requirement, decision or determination of the administrator or to decide in favor of the applicant any matter upon which it is required to pass under any ordinance (including the issuance of a permit as authorized) or to grant any variance. All other actions of the board shall be taken by majority vote, a quorum being present.

(d)

Once a member is physically present at a board meeting, any subsequent failure to vote shall be recorded as an affirmative vote unless the member has been excused in accordance with subsection (c) or has been allowed to withdraw from the meeting in accordance with subsection (f).

(e)

Board of adjustment members exercising quasi-judicial functions pursuant to the Cleveland County Unified Development Ordinance shall not participate in or vote on any quasi-judicial matter in a manner that would violate affected persons' constitutional rights to an impartial decision maker. Impermissible violations of due process include, but are not limited to, a member having a fixed opinion prior to hearing the matter that is not susceptible to change, undisclosed ex parte communications, a close familial, business, or other associational relationship with an affected person, or a financial interest in the outcome of the matter.

(f)

A member may be allowed to withdraw from the remainder of a meeting by majority vote of the remaining members present for any good and sufficient reason other than the member's desire to avoid voting on matters to be considered at the meeting.

(g)

A motion to allow a member to be excused from voting or excused from the remainder of the meeting is in order only if made by or at the initiative of the member directly affected.

(h)

A roll call vote shall be taken upon the request of any member.

(i)

An objection to the inclusion or exclusion of administrative material will be decided by the board chair. An appeal of this decision will be voted on by the whole board.
