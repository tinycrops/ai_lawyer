(a)

Approval. Any person desiring to deposit or discharge any industrial waste into the county sewers shall make application to the county in the manner prescribed therefor. The county shall approve in writing such applications only when evidence is submitted by the applicant that the discharge into sewers will comply with this article and any regulation enacted pursuant thereto.

(b)

Excessive flows or characteristics.

(1)

Determination. In order to prevent overloading the sewage treatment plant by discharges of wastes at high rates or by discharges having excessive biological or chemical characteristics, the county shall determine from time to time if one or more of the following conditions are present with respect to the deposit or discharge of such person:

a.

The biological (biochemical) oxygen demand is in excess of 250 parts per million.

b.

The pH is lower than 6.0 or higher than 9.0 for a continuous period of 20 minutes.

c.

The suspended solids are in excess of 250 parts per million.

d.

Either the average rate of flow or the peak rate of flow is in excess of the treatment plant's capacity for such flows, respectively; referred to as "excessive flow" in this division.

e.

The chemical oxygen demand is in excess of 1,200 parts per million.

(2)

Reduction. After such determination, the county shall notify such persons to cease such discharges or to take one or more of the following steps to alleviate the condition:

a.

In the case of a person whose peak rate of flow is excessive, but whose average rate of flow is not excessive, construct a storage tank, holding pond or other device which, with suitable flow control, will equalize the flow over a period of 24 hours.

b.

Treat his wastes to prevent the flow from exceeding the limit of pH stated in subsection (b)(1)b of this section.

c.

Reduce the biological (biochemical) oxygen demand of his wastes to such a level that the biological (biochemical) oxygen demand is not in excess of 250 parts per million.

d.

Reduce the suspended solids of his wastes to such an extent that the suspended solids of the flow is not in excess of 250 parts per million.

e.

In the case of a person whose average rate of flow is excessive, reduce such flow to a nonexcessive level.

f.

Reduce COD to 1,200 parts per million.

(c)

Excessive characteristics may be allowed under special approval. At the discretion of the county and with the approval of the board of commissioners, in the case where compliance with subsection (c) of this section would create undue hardship upon the person discharging waste with excessive characteristics, the county may execute a special written approval with such person whereby he will be permitted to continue discharge of his wastes uncontrolled in whole or in part. Such permit shall provide for such surcharges as may be determined to compensate the county for treating the uncontrolled wastes. Determination of the amount of surcharges shall be made by application of the principles set forth in division 6 of this article.

(d)

Control manhole. Any person discharging industrial wastes into the sewer system shall construct and maintain a suitable control manhole downstream from any treatment, storage or other approved works, to facilitate observation, measurement and sampling of all wastes, including domestic sewage, from the industry. The control manhole shall be constructed in a manner approved by the county.
