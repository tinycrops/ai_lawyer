(a)

Where, because of the size of the tract to be subdivided, its topography, the condition or nature of adjoining areas or the existence of other unusual physical conditions, strict compliance with the provisions of the chapter would cause an unusual and unnecessary hardship on the subdivider, the board of adjustment may vary the requirements set forth herein. In granting variances, the board of adjustment may require such conditions as will secure, insofar as practicable, the objectives of the requirement varied. Any variance authorized shall be entered in the minutes of the board of adjustment, together with the circumstances that justified the variance granted, and the conditions upon which the variance was granted. Submission of a variance application shall be accompanied by a filing fee set forth in the schedule of fees adopted by the board of commissioners.

(b)

Any person that is aggrieved by the decision of the board of adjustment may, within 30 days of the board of adjustment's decision, petition the board of commissioners to review the board of adjustment's decision. The board of commissioners may uphold the action of the board of adjustment or make any other decision with reference to the variance application that it deems appropriate.
