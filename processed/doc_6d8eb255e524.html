In order for farmland to qualify for inclusion into the voluntary agricultural district or the enhanced voluntary agricultural district, it must be real property that:

(1)

Is engaged in agriculture as that word is defined in G.S. 106-581.1.1.

(2)

Be the subject of a conservation agreement as defined in G.S. 121-35, between the county and the owner of such land that prohibits non-farm use or development of such land for at least ten years, except for the creation of not more than three lots that meet applicable county zoning and subdivision regulations.

(3)

Agree to the conservation agreement between Cabarrus County and the owner that the enhanced voluntary agricultural district cannot be revoked for at least ten years.

(Ord. No. 2005-20, Art. VII, 11-21-05; Ord. No. 2016-04, 3-21-16)
