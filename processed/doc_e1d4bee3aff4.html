(a)

Abandoned vehicles generally.

(1)

Upon the removal and storage of any abandoned vehicle from any state-owned, county-owned or private property, written notice by mail shall be promptly given to the registered owner of the vehicle. Such owner, before obtaining possession of the vehicle, shall pay the county all reasonable costs incidental to the removal and storage of the vehicle and of locating its owner.

(2)

Except as provided for junk vehicles in subsection (b) of this section, if the identity or whereabouts of the owner of an abandoned vehicle cannot be ascertained, the official designated to remove and store the vehicle shall dispose of the vehicle by public or private sale after holding it for thirty (30) days, having its value determined by three (3) disinterested dealers or garagemen, and after twenty (20) days' notice to the department of motor vehicles. The proceeds of such sale shall be forwarded to the treasurer of the county. The treasurer shall pay the following from the proceeds of the sale, in the following order:

a.

The costs of removal, storage, and investigation as to the owner;

b.

The costs of sale; and

c.

As far as possible from the remaining proceeds, if any, any lien that may be recorded against the vehicle.

Any funds remaining shall be deposited in the general fund of the county, to be paid to the owner of the vehicle upon proper proof of ownership.

(b)

Junk vehicles. If an abandoned motor vehicle is found to be inoperable, dismantled or damaged, five (5) years old or older, and worth less than twenty-five dollars ($25.00), it shall be declared to be a junk motor vehicle. If such a vehicle is removed to a storage area or garage, it shall be held for at least fifteen (15) days. During this time it may be reclaimed by its owner, upon the owner's establishing proof of ownership to the satisfaction of the official designated to remove and store the vehicle, and paying all reasonable costs incidental to the vehicle's removal and storage. If a junk vehicle remains unclaimed after fifteen (15) days, the junk vehicle may be destroyed or disposed of as salvage. Any funds derived from the disposition of junk motor vehicles shall be deposited in the general fund of the county. Within fifteen (15) days after final disposition, notice shall be given to the department of motor vehicles that such vehicle has been disposed of as a junk motor vehicle. With the consent of the owner of a junk motor vehicle, the vehicle may be removed and disposed of, regardless of its value, condition or age, without holding it for the fifteen-day period set out in this subsection.
