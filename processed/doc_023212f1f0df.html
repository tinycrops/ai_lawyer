The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:

Check valve means a device installed in water lines to restrict flow in one direction.

Developer and subdivider mean any person who subdivides or develops any land deemed to be a development or subdivision.

Development and subdivision mean all divisions of a tract or parcel of land into two or more lots, building sites or other divisions for the purpose of sale or building development, whether immediate or future, and include all division of land involving the dedication of a new street or a change in existing streets; provided, however, the following are not included:

(1)

The division of land parcels greater than ten acres if no street right-of-way dedication is involved; and

(2)

The division of a tract in single ownership, the entire area of which is no greater than two acres, into not more than three lots, if no street right-of-way dedication is involved.

Domestic sewage means liquid or water-carried waste from bathrooms, toilet rooms, kitchens and home laundries.

Garbage means solid wastes from the handling, storage and sale of produce.

Industrial waste means liquid or water-carried waste from institutional, commercial and industrial processes and operations, as distinguished from domestic sewage.

Lateral means a connection between a water main and a meter and is the same as a tap.

Meter means any device used to measure the flow of water.

Peak flow means the maximum rate of flow occurring at any time measured in terms of gallons per normal calendar day or 24 hours.

pH means the logarithm (base ten) of the reciprocal of the hydrogen ion concentration expressed in moles per liter, and indicates the degree of acidity or alkalinity of the substance. A pH value of 7.0 is neutral, being neither acid or alkaline. Values below 7.0 are acid and those above 7.0 are alkaline.

ppm means parts per million by weight in water and sewage analysis, the equivalent of milligrams per liter.

Premises means:

(1)

Each residential dwelling unit, industrial unit or mobile home and any lot or space to be used for a trailer or mobile home.

(2)

Each unit contained in any structure serving a separate owner, tenant or lessee and used for any purpose other than for residential use.

Properly shredded garbage means the waste from the preparation, cooking and dispensing of food that has been shredded to such degree that all particles will be carried freely under flow conditions normally prevailing in public sewer piping, with no particles greater than one-half inch in diameter.

Public sanitary sewer and public sewer mean a sewer owned and/or operated, maintained and controlled by the county which conveys domestic, industrial, commercial or institutional wastewaters.

Sewage means a combination of wastewaters from residences, commercial, industrial and institutional establishments.

Sewer connection or lateral means a sewer pipeline, normally of four-inch minimum diameter, running laterally from a gravity street sewer, an off-street sewer or a trunk sewer to an individual tract, lot, parcel of land to serve one or more houses for other buildings, whether or not connected to any house or building. The sewer connection or lateral generally means the same as sewer tap.

Sewer tap means the connection pipe from the public gravity sewer line to the property line of an individual tract, lot or parcel of land to serve one or more houses or other buildings.

Storm sewer and storm drain mean a sewer or drain designed and constructed to carry stormwaters and surface waters and drainage, but not sewage and polluted wastewaters.

Suspended solids means solids that either float on the surface of or are in suspension in water, sewage or other liquids or wastewaters, which are removable by methods of filtering, settling, etc., used by wastewater disposal plants, sewage treatment plants or waste treatment plants. Suspended solids (SS) are normally expressed in parts per million (ppm) or milligrams per liter (mg/l) by dry weight.

Tap means a service connection between the water main and the meter and meter box, including the meter and meter box.

Transmission pipe or main means a water pipe laid along major thoroughfares and/or along roadways connecting major thoroughfares. Mains are larger than eight inches in diameter.

Valve cock, valve, gate valve and stop cock mean a device to stop or restrict the flow of water in lines.

Water main means any water pipes run underground in streets, indicated rights-of-way or easements carrying water to various areas. Water mains are usually six inches in diameter or larger.
