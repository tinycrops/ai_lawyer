(a)

Creation. There is created an environmental affairs board for the county.

(b)

Membership. Members on the environmental affairs board should include a cross section of citizens and professions of the county. The membership of the board shall be 11 members and will consist of the following: one county commissioner, four at-large citizens, two representatives of private industry, one representative of the medical community, one public utility representative, one person representing the building or real estate trade, and one municipal representative.

(c)

Duties. The duties of the environmental affairs board include advising the board of commissioners on environmental policy, educating the public and local officials, performing special studies and projects, facilitating citizen participation, maintaining awareness of changes in environmental laws and regulations affecting the county, and promoting state/county, public and private, cooperation and coordination. The duties may be changed periodically at the discretion of the county board of commissioners. The scope of issues the environmental affairs board can examine is wide and includes, but is not limited to, topics such as hazardous and radioactive materials management and disposal, watershed protection, groundwater and surface water protection, erosion control, air quality, energy conservation, incineration, pollution prevention, waste management, county beautification and recycling.
