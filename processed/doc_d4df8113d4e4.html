(a)

For the purposes of this article, a vehicle shall be determined to have been abandoned if the vehicle:

(1)

Has been left upon county-owned property or public grounds within the county in violation of a law or ordinance prohibiting parking;

(2)

Fails to display a current license plate;

(3)

Is partially dismantled or wrecked;

(4)

Is incapable of self-propulsion or being moved in the manner for which it was originally intended;

(5)

Is left on property owned or operated by the county for a period of not less than twenty-four (24) hours;

(6)

Is left on private property without the consent of the owner, occupant or lessee thereof for a period of not less than twenty-four (24) hours; or

(7)

Is left on any public grounds within the county for a period of not less than seven (7) days.

(b)

Nothing in this article shall be construed to apply to:

(1)

Any vehicle in an enclosed building;

(2)

Any vehicle on the premises of a business enterprise being operated in a lawful place and manner, where the vehicle is necessary to the operation of such business enterprise; or

(3)

Any vehicle in an appropriate storage place or depository maintained in a lawful place and manner by the county.
