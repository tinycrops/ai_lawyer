The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:

Domestic septage means either liquid or solid material removed from a septic tank, cesspool, portable toilet, type III marine sanitation device, or similar treatment works receiving only domestic sewage. The term "domestic septage" does not include liquid or solid material removed from a septic tank, cesspool, or similar treatment works receiving either commercial wastewater or industrial wastewater and does not include grease removed from a grease trap at a restaurant.

Health department means the county health department as authorized to be organized under state law.

Industrial or commercial septage means material pumped from septic tanks or other devices used in the collection, pretreatment, or treatment of any water-carried waste resulting from any process of industry, manufacture, trade, or business where the design disposal of the wastewater is subsurface. Domestic septage mixed with any industrial or commercial septage is considered industrial or commercial septage.

Owner/operator means the individual who owns or is responsible for management and/or day-to-day operations of the septage management firm.

Septage means a solid waste that is a fluid mixture of untreated and partially treated sewage solids, liquids, and sludge of human or domestic origin which is removed from septic tanks; also septage as defined in G.S. 130A-290(a)(32); and includes washings from the interior of septage handling containers, including pumper trucks.

Septage management firm means a person involved in the activity of pumping, transporting, storing, treating or disposing of septage. The term does not include public or community sanitary sewer systems that treat or dispose of septage. The owner/operator shall be responsible for ensuring the firm's compliance with all statutory and regulatory requirements.
