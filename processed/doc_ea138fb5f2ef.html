As chief administrative officer, it shall be the power or duty of the county manager to:

(1)

Direct and supervise the administration of all county officials and departments for which the board of commissioners is responsible, or may become responsible, including specifically the maintenance and operation of all county buildings and other public works, and the care and maintenance of all personal property owned by the county;

(2)

Administer personnel policies, purchase all supplies and equipment, finance, accounting, budgeting, payroll, auditing and any other administrative responsibilities necessary for implementation of the board's policies;

(3)

Appoint, suspend and remove all county officers, employees and agents, except the county attorney, those who are elected by the people, and those whose appointment is otherwise provided by law; and delegate to any department head such powers with respect to subordinates within the officer's department or office;

(4)

See that all ordinances, resolutions and orders of the board of commissioners and all laws of the state subject to enforcement by him or by officers or department heads subject, under this division, to his direction and supervision are faithfully executed;

(5)

Prepare and submit the annual budget and capital program to the board of commissioners and execute the budget and capital program adopted by the board, approving all disbursements and expenditures as budgeted and/or authorized by the board;

(6)

Confer with and assist the heads of departments, such as the county health department and the county department of social services, the functions of which are not under the direct control and supervision of the board of county commissioners, but which are dependent upon appropriations of the board of commissioners for the continued operation of such departments;

(7)

Submit periodically to the board of commissioners a department program performance report which presents a quantifiable summary of work activities performed by each agency and which shows the extent to which such activities meet program goals and objectives;

(8)

Confer and advise with all other elected or appointed officials of the county who are not under the immediate control of the board but who receive financial support from the board, such as judges, the sheriff, and register of deeds;

(9)

Keep the board of commissioners fully advised on the financial condition of the future needs of the county;

(10)

Make such recommendations on county affairs as he deems necessary for the efficient operation of the county;

(11)

Submit to the board of commissioners at the end of each fiscal year a complete report on the finances and administrative activities of the county for the preceding year; and

(12)

Carry into execution such other powers and duties as may be prescribed from time to time by the board of commissioners.
