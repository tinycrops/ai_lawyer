(a)

Projects eligible for the revenue sharing program are limited to:

(1)

Projects submitted through a governmental entity.

(2)

Projects within the county.

(3)

Projects outside of an incorporated area where annexation will not occur for at least two years.

(4)

Projects supported by information sufficient to allow for adequate evaluation of the project.

(5)

Projects having at least one of the following criteria:

a.

Associated with water and sewer line extensions for the purpose of offering utility service which could include, but not limited to, storage or treatment facilities, only if the storage or treatment facilities are identified as necessary to accommodate water and/or sewer needed in the county.

b.

Constructed to stimulate economic development or accommodate industrial or commercial growth.

c.

Addresses environmental needs such as the health and safety of the general public.

(b)

Project costs eligible for the revenue sharing program are limited to:

(1)

The actual cost of design and construction of the work described in the project application.

(2)

Contingency costs, not to exceed ten percent of the eligible construction estimate; however, upon acceptance of bid, the allowed contingency cost may be reduced to five percent. If the municipality determines upsizing of a line is necessary to accommodate future growth, the cost figures submitted must represent the larger line size.

(c)

Ineligible project costs for the revenue sharing program include, but are not limited to:

(1)

Administrative costs.

(2)

Recurring expenditures associated with operations and maintenance.

(3)

Portions funded through federal, state, and local grants. Such funds must be directly deducted from the project costs.

(4)

In-kind services rendered by either party.

(d)

No maximum is established for the revenue sharing project amounts. Projects will be evaluated and funded according to information supplied and available funds.

(Ord. No. 2006-07, 6-15-2006; Ord. No. 2017-11, 9-18-2017; Ord. No. 2020-17, 10-19-2020)
