    About the American Law Wiki

    Overview

    The American Law Wiki is an AI-powered repository of legal documents from across the United States. This system processes and structures legal texts to make them more accessible and usable.

    Using advanced natural language processing techniques, the system:

    • Analyzes the structure of legal documents
    • Identifies sections, paragraphs, and references
    • Creates a consistent JSON schema for each document type
    • Provides a searchable web interface to browse the documents

    Processing Pipeline

    1. Data Collection: Documents are collected from the American Law dataset.
    2. Schema Analysis: AI identifies the document structure.
    3. Parsing Methodology: Custom parsing rules are generated.
    4. Document Processing: Raw documents are transformed into structured data.
    5. Validation: Results are checked for consistency and completeness.
    6. Storage: Processed documents are saved in a standardized format.

    Technology Stack

    • Backend: Python with Flask
    • Database: SQLite with SQLAlchemy ORM
    • AI Processing: Google Gemini API
    • Frontend: Bootstrap 5, HTML/CSS/JavaScript
    • Data Source: HuggingFace American Law Dataset
